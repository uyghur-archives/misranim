“Baghlaqsiz Melodi” eng guzel muhabet nahshillirining biri. Bu nahshining muzikisini 1955-yili Alex North yazghan, lirikisini 1936-yili Hy Zaret yazghan. Bu nahsha 20-esirdiki eng kop lintige elinghan nahshilarning birige aylanghan. Bezi molcherlerde bu nahshining 500 tin artuq nus’hillirining neche yuz ohshimighan tilda lintigha elinghanlighi tehmin qilinmaqta.
1955 Yili muzikand Alex North bu nahshini turme hayati heqqidiki ishlen’gen “baghlaqsiz” digen kino tima nahshisi qilip yazghan. Nahshining ismi shu kinoning ismidin kelgen. Todd Duncan u kinodiki nahshini oqughan. Undin kiyin bu nahshini bir qanche artistlar oqughan, emma 1965-yili The Righteous Brothers (Heq-qani aka-ukilar) muzika etriti eyitqan nus’hisi 20-esirning ahirlirida olchemge aylinip qalghan. Bulupmu 1990-yilda chiqqan dangliq kino “Jin”da mu muzika timu muzikisi qilinghandin biri tehimu dangliq bolup ketken.
Bu nahsha dunyadiki asasliq tillarning hemmiside, jumlidin Hanzuchidimu eytilghan. Bir kuni Uyghurchidimu eytilip baqsa ejep emes.

"Unchained Melody" is a 1955 song with music by Alex North and lyrics by Hy Zaret who wrote it in 1936. It has become one of the most recorded songs of the 20th century, by some estimates having spawned over 500 versions in hundreds of different languages.[1]
In 1955, Alex North used the music as a theme for the prison film Unchained, hence the name. Todd Duncan sang the vocals for the film soundtrack. Several artists released their version of the song, but it was the July 1965 version by The Righteous Brothers that became a jukebox standard for the late 20th century, regaining massive popularity when used in the 1990 blockbuster film Ghost.
